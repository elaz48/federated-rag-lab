# Meridian Analytics — Product Overview

Meridian Analytics is a B2B SaaS platform for mid-market and enterprise teams that need a single place to turn operational data into decisions. Customers connect warehouses, CRMs, and billing systems; Meridian normalizes the streams and surfaces KPIs, anomaly alerts, and narrative summaries that non-technical stakeholders can act on.

## Core capabilities

**Unified metrics layer.** Define once, reuse everywhere. Metric definitions (revenue, churn, utilization, SLA attainment) live in a governed catalog so finance, product, and ops stop arguing over which spreadsheet is correct.

**Live dashboards.** Drag-and-drop boards with role-based views. Boards refresh on a schedule or on event triggers. Viewers can drill into a chart without writing SQL.

**Anomaly detection.** Statistical and rule-based monitors watch key metrics. When a signal drifts, Meridian opens an incident card with the contributing dimensions (segment, region, product line) so on-call staff can triage quickly.

**Narrative briefings.** Scheduled digests explain *what changed and why* in plain language. Briefings can be emailed, posted to Slack, or opened inside the product.

**Governed exports and API.** Professional and Enterprise plans expose a REST API and advanced export formats. Starter users export CSV from individual charts only.

## Who it is for

Meridian is built for revenue operations, customer success leadership, and data teams that support them. Typical deployments have 20–500 seats. Integrations ship first-class connectors for Snowflake, BigQuery, HubSpot, Salesforce, Stripe, and Postgres; custom HTTP sources are available on Enterprise.

## What Meridian is not

Meridian is not a general-purpose BI replacement for heavy ad-hoc analysis, and it is not a data warehouse. It sits on top of systems of record and focuses on trusted operational metrics, alerts, and stakeholder communication.
